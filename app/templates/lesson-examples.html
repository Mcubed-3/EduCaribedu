Example 1

Photosynthesis (Grade 8 Science)

Curriculum area: Integrated Science / Biology foundations

Lesson focus: Understanding how plants produce food and why photosynthesis is essential for life.

Grade / Level Grade 8
Structure 5Es
Difficulty Intermediate
Duration 60 minutes

Specific objectives

  • Define photosynthesis in simple scientific language.
  • Identify sunlight, carbon dioxide, water, and chlorophyll as key parts of the process.
  • Explain why photosynthesis is important for plant growth and life on Earth.

Suggested lesson flow

  • Engage: Ask students how plants get food if they cannot move around like animals.
  • Explore: Observe a leaf diagram and identify where sunlight and gases are involved.
  • Explain: Teacher explains the process of photosynthesis using a labeled visual.
  • Elaborate: Students work in pairs to sequence the stages of the process.
  • Evaluate: Students complete a short written explanation of how plants make food.

Resources

  • Leaf diagram or plant chart
  • Whiteboard and markers
  • Short science video or teacher explanation

Assessment

Students complete a short worksheet labeling the parts of the photosynthesis process and writing two to three sentences about why the process matters.

Example 2

Fractions (Grade 5 Mathematics)

Curriculum area: Number concepts and operations

Lesson focus: Building understanding of basic fraction addition and subtraction using models and guided practice.

Grade / Level Grade 5
Structure 5Es
Difficulty Beginner to Intermediate
Duration 60 minutes

Specific objectives

  • Recognize fractions as equal parts of a whole.
  • Add and subtract simple fractions with like denominators.
  • Use visual fraction models to explain answers.

Suggested lesson flow

  • Engage: Use everyday examples such as pizza or fruit slices to introduce fractions.
  • Explore: Students shade fraction bars or circles to represent simple fractions.
  • Explain: Teacher models addition and subtraction of like fractions step by step.
  • Elaborate: Students solve guided practice questions in pairs.
  • Evaluate: Students complete individual fraction problems and explain one answer.

Resources

  • Fraction strips or circles
  • Worksheet with visual models
  • Board examples for guided instruction

Assessment

Students complete a short exercise with fraction addition and subtraction questions and explain how a visual model helped them find one answer.

Example 3

Persuasive Writing (Grade 9 English)

Curriculum area: Writing and communication

Lesson focus: Using reasons, examples, and structure to write a persuasive paragraph or short essay.

Grade / Level Grade 9
Structure 4Cs
Difficulty Intermediate
Duration 60 minutes

Specific objectives

  • Identify the features of persuasive writing.
  • Develop a clear opinion supported by reasons and examples.
  • Write a persuasive response using organized paragraph structure.

Suggested lesson flow

  • Connect: Discuss an everyday topic students have strong opinions about.
  • Construct: Review a model persuasive paragraph and identify key features.
  • Contemplate: Students plan reasons and supporting details for a chosen topic.
  • Continue: Students draft and revise a persuasive paragraph or short essay.

Resources

  • Sample persuasive text
  • Planning organizer
  • Writing prompt and success criteria

Assessment

Students submit a persuasive paragraph or short essay showing a clear opinion, logical reasons, and relevant supporting details.
